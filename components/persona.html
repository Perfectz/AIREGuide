The 5-Step Persona Playbook

Follow this proven framework to create actionable buyer personas that drive results

1. Feed the Machine

This is where you give the AI the raw materials. Don't just give it scraps; give it a feast of data. Modern AI can search the web, so you can point it toward public information. Combine multiple sources for the best results:

  • Your Own Data: Anonymized information from your past clients. What were their jobs? What neighborhoods did they buy in? What were their biggest questions?
  • Public Data: Tell the AI to analyze demographic data for a specific zip code from the US Census Bureau, or local market reports from your MLS.
  • Social Media Insights: Ask the AI to research public LinkedIn profiles for common job titles in your target area (e.g., "Analyze public LinkedIn profiles for 'Software Engineer' in Alpharetta, GA to understand their career levels and interests"). It can also analyze trends in local Facebook groups or on real estate forums.

Example: To build a "First-Time Buyer" persona: Feed it data on average rent prices, median income for 25-35 year olds in your city, and common questions from your blog's FAQ page.

Example: To build a "Luxury Seller" persona: Feed it data on high-end home sales in Buckhead, common amenities in those homes, and ask it to analyze public profiles of executives at major Atlanta corporations.

2. Give Them a Name

Tell the AI: "Suggest 3-4 buyer persona categories with catchy names." "Urban Starter." "Upsizing Family." Make them real.

3. Map Their Pain & Gain

Prompt: "What are the biggest worries and greatest hopes for an 'Upsizing Family'?" This is about emotion, not features.

4. Live a Day in Their Life

The empathy step. "Write a 1-day narrative of them home-shopping." Feel their frustration. Understand their dream.

5. Build Your Battle Card

Condense it all into a one-page profile. This is your targeting guide for all marketing.

Example Personal Voice Rules

Here's what your AI-generated voice rules might look like

Sarah's Voice Rules (Example)

Be Direct & Actionable

Use clear, specific language. Instead of "Let's talk about your options," say "Here are your 3 best next steps." Always provide concrete next actions.

Lead with Confidence

Use phrases like "I know this market" and "Here's what I'm seeing." Show expertise without being arrogant. Your confidence builds client trust.

Make It Personal

Connect every property feature to lifestyle benefits. "This kitchen isn't just granite counters‚Äîit's where your family will gather for Sunday brunches."

‚ö° Create Urgency (But Be Honest)

Use phrases like "This won't last long" and "I'm showing it to 3 other buyers this week," but only when it's true. Authentic urgency, not fake scarcity.

Be Their Advocate

Position yourself as their champion: "I'm fighting for your best interests" and "Let me handle the tough conversations." Show you're on their team.

Pro Tip: These rules become your AI prompt add-ons. When writing listings, add "Follow Sarah's Voice Rules" to keep your content authentically you.
